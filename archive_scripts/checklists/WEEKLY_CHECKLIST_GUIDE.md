# Weekly Client Checklist - User Guide

## Purpose

This standardized checklist ensures all engineers:
- Capture scope changes systematically
- Identify client satisfaction issues early
- Track project health consistently
- Generate meaningful weekly summaries for clients and management

---

## How to Use This Checklist

### During Client Meetings/Calls

1. **Keep the checklist open** during your weekly client sync
2. **Check boxes as you go** - quick and easy during conversation
3. **Add brief notes** where needed (don't write essays)
4. **Complete any unchecked items** after the call from memory/notes

### Between Client Meetings

- Use the checklist as a **conversation guide** if you don't have regular meetings
- Send the key questions via email and fill in responses
- Review project status weekly even without direct client contact

---

## Completing the Checklist

### Time Required
- **During meeting:** 0 minutes (just check boxes as you talk)
- **After meeting:** 5-10 minutes to add notes and complete

## Red Flags to Escalate Immediately

Even if it's mid-week, flag these to your PM immediately:

ðŸš¨ **Urgent Escalation Items:**
- Client expressing serious dissatisfaction
- Scope change that's >20% of project value
- Client making significant out-of-scope requests
- Project health going from Green to Red in one week
- Client threatening to stop work or withhold payment
- Safety or compliance issues
- Deadline that cannot be met

This template can be adapted for your specific project:

- **Add stakeholders** if you have multiple client contacts

- Scope changes (Section 2)
- Client satisfaction (Section 4)
- Overall health (Section 10)
